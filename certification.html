Course Completion

MOOCs have grown in number and popularity but failed to live up to its mission of democratizing education around the world. Out of the 290948 students who registered for the HarvardX open courseware in 2012 and 2013, only 65,261 completed the courseware representing 22.4% of total student enrollnments. Out of the 65,261 who completed the courseware only 5728 received certifications, distressingly representing 2% of total student enrollnments.

Certification

Certification rate in general is disappointingly low but we want to see if this is true for all demographics in this dataset. We will analyze individual courses, student age, level of education as well as the country of origin to perhaps diagnose the problem of low completion and low certification. First, out of 143,266 students who registered for Introduction to Computer Science, only 1% were certified. Out of 49,293 students who registered for Justice, 4% were certified. Out of 37709 students who registered for Health in numbers, 5% were certified. Out of the 35509 students who registered for Human health and global environmental change, 2% were certified. Out of the 25171 students who registered for the Ancient Greek hero, only 1% received certifications.

Secondly, about 73% of the students who completed the courseware and received certification already have a bachelor's, master's or doctorate degree(s). This shows that students accessing the courseware regard it merely as a resource to fill in gaps to suplement skills already learnt in a four year traditional institution. Additionally about 67% of those who recieved certifications fall within ages 16 to 30 which is the targeted age group, the only problem is that there are simple not enough certifications for students within this age group compared to the number of enrollments.

8 out of the top 9 countries by certification are from Europe with the exception of one country from South Asia which is India.The top 9 countries make up 45% of all certifications from the HarvardX open courseware.
